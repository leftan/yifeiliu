The Challenge

A Furtune 100 company came to us to redesign its flagship B2B ecommerce website. As the 2nd largest ecommerce site in the world (after Amazon), the product brings $50B of annual revenue to the company and has a unique market advantage over its competitors.

The Solution

Based on extensive user research, we crafted concept designs to transform the product into a seamless ecommerce experience of hardware, software, and services for the next generation of enterprise merchants.

Design Research

User Interviews

We interviewed more than 30 users across 5 countries in 3 continents. We asked them about their behaviors, pain points, frustrations and desires. We further categorized the research findings into 4 groups: delighters, broken functionalities, incremental opportunities, and wishlists.

Some of the Key Insights

“I need to remember everything.”

Each purchase order is treated as an independent transaction. Since there is no end customer profile, users have to remember end customer’s preferences even if they forget to ask.

“I have to do this every single time.”

Simple updates of merchandise require users to go through the entire pricing approval process again. They must re-enter the same information and complete the same steps which cause an ample amount of frustration.

“I have no idea how to fix this.”

Users receive a number of error messages when configuring a product or service, but the error messages only indicate what is not working, not how to fix the problem.

Personas

Based on the research, we created 8 personas to represent different user groups.

Journey Map

The team mapped out an entire end-to-end user journey with touchpoints of all personas and their pain points. The journey map served as a springboard for the actual design work.

Conceptual Design

We created a new conceptual model, which is a design blueprint that outlines the new organizational structure of the system. We used it as a framework to list out major objects, and mark the connection between different objects. We also worked with the client to understand all those connections and come up with a business strategy and implementation plan that is the best fit based on the client’s needs.

Design Opportunity

How might we provide a simple and guided solution tailored to the end customer’s needs?

We’d like to provide more contextual information and intelligent assistance to help the users to make sense of the end customer’s requirements and come up with a suitable solution.

How might we provide an intuitive and seamless experience to configure a complex BOM?

We’d like to combine the configuration of both hardware and software/subscription (the curreny system has them separately) and show more visual aids to explain the complexity and help choose the right products.

Some Design Highlights

Design Impact

We have been working closely with the directors, product managers, and engineers throughout the whole process on this highly visible project. The client has shown great enthusiasm in the redesign concept and believes the new design concept would make it easy for millions of users to sell more products and boost the company’s revenues by providing the best suitable solutions to the end customers. The next design phase is currently on hold as the client is in the process of securing more fundings.
