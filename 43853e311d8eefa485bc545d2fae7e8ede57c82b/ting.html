TingTing

Enhance Family Driving Experience and Engage with the Little Passengers

At a Glance

Duration

3 Months

Team

2 Designers

My Role

UX/UI Designer

Context

The rise of digital technologies has brought huge improvements in mobility. From on-demand rideshare platforms like Uber and Lyft to bus route optimization algorithms to train arrival APIs, getting around is easier. Sadly, many of these improvements have failed to make getting around easier or more enjoyable for families. A lot of families still choose to drive. However, with young children on board, distraction becomes stressful.

Why it matters?

Getting distracted is a common behavior when driving, and it causes around 8,000 accidents every day in the US. Some common distraction factors include cell-phone use, navigation and other non-driving related like eating or drinking. However, parent drivers are at a higher risk due to child related distractions, which is often neglected. Child-related distraction makes parent’s crash rate 3 times higher than the general population and nearly as high as teenage drivers.

The Solution

We completely redesigned the platform from scratch to make the product simpler and more intuitive while maintaining the core features of the product.

Design Research

We conducted 16 semi-structured interviews with parents to understand their commuting and driving experience with children on board. We also conducted 3 parent journey mapping exercises to understand their trips from preparation to destination and how trips differed from weekdays and weekends.

Research Insights

1. Parents are often stressed to entertain their kids with unique interests, especially in a rush.
Unfortunately, parents are constantly in a rush, especially among working parents and parents with multiple children. In addition, kids have very particular interest about what they want now as they are growing up with various entertainment channel and content options.

2. Kids becoming curious about the driving context can turn into a burden of occupied parents.
As kids get older, they start to ask questions about their surroundings. Some parents even say that their kids sometimes acts like backseat drivers when they are driving. This will make parents even more stressed.

3. Dilemma: to talk or not to talk?

Parents encounter the dilemma of whether to start a conversation wit their kids or not. For example, some times parents are really tired after work and don’t want to initiate a conversation, yet they feel guilty just let their kids sit there and watch iPad in the back.

Conceptual Design

Based on the insights, we designed a conceptual audio app that aims to enhance safe driving for parents and increase engagement for kids. It's called TingTing, which means listening in Mandarin.

Learn New Stuff

Get your kids excited about the world by providing personalized podcast recommendations.

It’s Storytime

Open kids’ imagination with interactive stories and initiate story-related conversations.

Go on Adventure

Discover city history & fun facts about your trip by tracking the geo-location of the car.

Why Audio?

1. Open imagination

Audio content leaves out a lot of direct visual clues, and it allows kids to fill in the blank with imagination.

2. Family bonding

Audio content can bond families together. Families can experience “communal listening” that are interesting for all ages.

3. Reduce screen time

Parents are concerned about screen time for many reasons. For example, looking at screens in a moving vehicle for too long might cause carsick. Looking at screens in a low lighting environment could be bad for the eyes.

Design Impact

This design concept aimed to find a delicate balance between driver’s attention, kids’ entertainment and their interaction in the car, at the same time, take different driving context into consideration.Think about a scenario when multiple kids are in the car. TingTing can help facilitate conversations between the kids based on their common interests. In a self-driving car era, when driving requires less attention, TingTing can also interact with parents and provide communal entertainment that everyone enjoys.

Disclaimer: this school project didn’t get implemented.
