Google Pets

A mobile app that helps pets find new home

At a Glance

The Challenge

Millions of animals are currently in shelters and foster homes awaiting adoption. Design an experience that will help connect people looking for a new pet with the right companion for them. Help an adopter find a pet which matches their lifestyle, considering factors including breed, gender, age, temperament, and health status. Provide a high-level flow and supporting wire frames.

The Solution

I designed a mobile app that matches people who are interested in adopting with pets that meet their needs.

Research

User Surveys

I started by conducting research on how people adopt dogs and what are their needs, desires and pain points. I interviewed 8 friends and classmates who have adopted or considered adopting dogs before and asked about their adoption experience.

“I didn't realize at the time that there were other dog breeds that had the characteristics I was looking for- fluffy and small. If I had known, I might have broadened my search.”

Contextual Interviews & Shadowing

I visited two shelters in the East Bay area - Berkeley Animal Care Services and Berkeley Humane. I observed how people who are interested in adopting interacted with animals, volunteers, and staff members. I asked them their thought process in the adoption, and followed some visitors as they toured around the shelters.

Assumptions

Based on my research, I made a few assumptions on the pet adoption process.

  1. It is more challenging to adopt dogs than adopting cats or other animals, because dogs have many different breeds, and they generally need more attentions and time spent together. Therefore I mainly focus on dog adoption.
  2. I chose mobile as the platform because people generally use their phones to browse and search for pets.
  3. Shelters are normally understaffed and they rely heavily on volunteers. They don't have the capacity to manage a new content platform besides their own websites. The mobile app will use API, web scraping and natural-language-processing (NLP) techniques to get pet/shelter data. The users of this mobile app are mainly pet adopters.

Key Insights

1. You cannot judge a book by its cover.

All dogs are individuals. Often time their looks don't equal to their behaviors or personalities. Some people may have a particular breed in mind but they may adopt other dogs that fit their lifestyles.

2. Family members (including cats and other dogs) have to be on the same page.

The top reason that dogs get return to the shelter after being adopted is that the new one doesn't get along with the old one. It is also important to figure out which family member is going to take care of the daily tasks for the dog.

3. House size & rental agreement are constraints.

Some people live in small apartments, which might not be suitable for adopting large pets. Some rental agreements also have restrictions on the pets allowed in the buildings.
